# ThunderBayAI Source Monitoring Layer

## Purpose
Turn external AI announcements and emerging tech signals into locally relevant ThunderBayAI content that builds authority, captures attention, and routes monetization into Frayze.

## Core Principle
Do not just repost announcements.
Transform them into:
- local interpretation
- practical implementation guidance
- personal-use relevance when useful
- business-use relevance when useful
- conversion pathways when intent exists

## Source Types To Monitor
### Tier 1 — Official AI announcements
- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- Google AI / Gemini
- Microsoft AI
- Nvidia
- Meta AI
- Hugging Face
- Stability AI
- notable robotics and automation companies

### Tier 2 — Product and capability shifts
- new voice features
- agent features
- search changes
- image / video generation improvements
- robotics milestones
- automation platforms
- AI coding / workflow tools
- AI browser / operator tools

### Tier 3 — Local or industry-relevant implications
- shifts that affect contractors
- shifts that affect restaurants
- shifts that affect home services
- shifts that affect automotive businesses
- shifts that affect real estate workflows
- tools that reduce admin, improve follow-up, or increase conversion

## Monitoring Inputs
Use any reliable mix of:
- RSS feeds
- official company blogs
- release notes
- newsroom pages
- developer update feeds
- product changelogs
- curated announcement sources when useful

## Content Transformation Rules
For each important announcement, consider creating one or more of these:

### 1. What this means for Thunder Bay
Format:
- what changed
- why it matters locally
- who should care
- business consequence
- next move

### 2. How to implement this yourself
Format:
- who this is for
- simple setup logic
- minimum viable stack
- where DIY breaks
- when to get help

### 3. Personal benefits of AI
Format:
- practical day-to-day use
- time savings
- communication benefits
- productivity benefits
- caution / limitations

### 4. Business and AI
Format:
- direct operational use cases
- likely ROI angles
- workflow fit
- implementation order
- CTA to audit or strategy

### 5. Robots / physical AI / embodied systems
Format:
- what changed
- what is still hype
- what local operators should watch
- who might be affected first
- what it means for work and operations over time

### 6. Industry-specific spinouts
Examples:
- what this means for Thunder Bay contractors
- what this means for local restaurants
- what this means for service businesses with weak follow-up

## Content Gate
Only publish when the item has at least one of:
- real local relevance
- real business consequence
- strong authority value
- strong internal-link support
- real conversion opportunity

Do not publish generic AI recap content.

## Monetization Layer
Where relevant, route content into:
- AI Opportunity Audit
- Workflow Map
- Strategy Call
- Website Audit
- CRM / Automation / SEO service pages

## Workflow
1. Monitor announcements and feeds
2. Identify meaningful items
3. Score by local relevance, business relevance, and authority value
4. Create local interpretation angle
5. Create implementation angle if possible
6. Create business or personal angle where useful
7. Add internal links to money pages and tools
8. Publish only if commercially or strategically useful

## Priority Topics To Watch
- AI voice and phone handling
- AI search and local SEO changes
- agent workflows
- lead follow-up automation
- CRM and pipeline automation
- scheduling and booking systems
- AI assistants for operators
- robotics and embodied AI
- website conversion and AI interaction patterns

## Desired Outcome
When a major AI announcement happens, ThunderBayAI should be one of the first useful local properties to answer:
- what happened
- what it means here
- what you can do with it
- whether it matters for your business or personal life
- what the next practical step should be
